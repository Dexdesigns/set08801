How to prepare Egusi Ijebu (Melon Seeds Soup)

Ingredient

  • 2 cups of ground Egusi (melon seeds)
  • 2 cups of spinach leaves, washed and chopped
  • 1 medium-sized onion, chopped
  • 2 medium-sized fresh tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (habanero peppers), chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup of palm oil
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 1 tablespoon of locust bean seasoning (Iru)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • Water

Instructions

  • Place the ground Egusi in a bowl and add enough water to form a thick paste. Set aside.
  • Heat the palm oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  • Add the chopped onion and sauté for 2-3 minutes until translucent.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes and peppers, and sauté for another 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are soft and the mixture has thickened.
  • Add the locust bean seasoning, seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir to combine.
  • Add 2 cups of water to the pot and bring to a boil.
  • Take small scoops of the Egusi paste and drop them into the pot, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming.
  • Add more water if necessary to achieve the desired consistency.
  • Cover the pot and allow the mixture to cook for 10-15 minutes until the Egusi is cooked and has a fluffy texture.
  • Add the chopped spinach leaves and stir to combine.
  • Cover the pot and cook for another 5 minutes until the spinach leaves have wilted and are tender.
  • Remove the pot from the heat and allow the Egusi Ijebu to cool slightly before serving with your favorite side dish, such as pounded yam, Eba, Amala or Rice.
