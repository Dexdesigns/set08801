How to Cook Nigerian Jollof Rice

Ingredient

  • 2 cups of long-grain rice
  • 3 medium-sized fresh tomatoes
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (habanero peppers)
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 onion
  • 3 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 cups of chicken stock or water

Instructions

  • Rinse the rice thoroughly and soak in water for 15-20 minutes. Drain the water and set aside.
  • Blend the tomatoes, scotch bonnet peppers, red bell pepper, and onion in a blender until smooth.
  • Heat up the vegetable oil in a pot over medium heat.
  • Add the blended tomato and pepper mixture into the pot, and stir for 5-10 minutes until most of the water has evaporated and the mixture has thickened.
  • Add the tomato paste, curry powder, thyme, seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir to combine.
  • Add the chicken stock or water and bring to a boil.
  • Add the rice and stir to combine. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pot.
  • Allow the rice to cook for about 20-25 minutes or until all the liquid has been absorbed and the rice is tender. Do not open the pot during this time to ensure that the steam stays inside the pot and cooks the rice properly.
  • Once the rice is cooked, turn off the heat and fluff the rice with a fork.
  • Serve hot with your favorite side dish, such as grilled chicken, fried plantains, or coleslaw.
