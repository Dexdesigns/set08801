How to Prepare Nigerian Puff Puff

Ingredient

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of warm water
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 1/4 cup of powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions

  • In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, yeast, and salt.
  • Add the warm water and vanilla extract, and mix until the batter is smooth and lump-free.
  • Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let the batter rest in a warm place for 1 hour or until it doubles in size.
  • After the batter has risen, heat vegetable oil in a deep pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  • Using a small cookie scoop or spoon, drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil, about 2-3 tablespoons at a time.
  • Fry the puff puff for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  • Use a slotted spoon or tongs to remove the puff puff from the hot oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  • Repeat with the remaining batter until all the puff puff are cooked.
  • Optional: Dust the puff puff with powdered sugar before serving.
  • Serve the puff puff warm as a snack or dessert.
